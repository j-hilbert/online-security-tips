Strengthen Your Online Security

Top 10 essential actions to protect your digital life.

Why Online Security Matters

In today's interconnected world, safeguarding your online presence is more critical than ever. From personal data breaches to phishing scams, the threats are constant. By taking a few proactive steps, you can significantly enhance your security posture and protect your privacy.

Top 10 Essential Security Actions

1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Never reuse passwords. Use a reputable password manager to generate and store complex, unique passwords for each of your accounts.

Why?

The reason having a unique password for every account is so important is simple: You have no way of knowing how secure your account information is being protected when it is stored by a third party. You may use a very strong password, but if you use it on all of your accounts, then if any vendor gets compromised, ALL of your accounts are compromised. Enormous lists of leaked credentials are available online for bad actors to utilize. Don't let the bad security practices of the gossip forum you joined in 2003 be the reason your bank account gets drained today.

Good passwords are hard to guess and not used anywhere else. The best passwords are long, random text strings containing upper- and lower-case letters, numbers, and special characters. More than just being hard to guess, these long, random, complex passwords also take a long time to brute force, and bad actors attempting to do so are more likely to be detected in the process. As humans, we are not good at remembering large quantities of random passwords. Since you should be using a unique password for every account, managing this yourself will get cumbersone very quickly. Use a password manager. They're worth every penny you might spend on them, and if you're a bit more techy, you can host your own for free.

Which one should I choose?

A good password manager stores only encrypted data, has abundant security mechanisms available to secure your personal information, and offers browser extensions and mobile apps that you can use to fill login credentials automatically as you need them. The more convenient it is to use, the more likely you are to use it. Because it allows access to ALL of your accounts, it is especially important that you secure it well. Enable multifactor authentication at the very least. More on that to come.

I like Bitwarden, personally, but there are many options available to choose from, including some from vendors you may already use.

  • Bitwarden:
    A great open-source and free or affordable option, Bitwarden provides end-to-end encryption and a secure vault for storing sensitive information. It also offers browser extensions and mobile apps.
  • 1Password:
    Known for its strong security and user-friendly interface, 1Password is a popular choice for individuals and businesses. It offers a wide range of features and robust security measures, including 2FA and local encryption.
  • Dashlane:
    Dashlane is known for its comprehensive feature set, including a VPN, password health checker, and dark web monitoring, in addition to strong security features like 2FA and 256-bit AES encryption.
  • Keeper:
    Keeper is lauded for its user-friendly interface and robust security features, including 2FA and a zero-knowledge security model where encryption and decryption happen on the user's device.
  • NordPass:
    This option is recognized for its strong encryption, user-friendly interface, and cross-platform capabilities.
  • LastPass:
    I mention LastPass here mostly to preemptively answer the question, "What about LastPass?" LastPass employees the features I mention above, but according to online sources, has a poor security track record, including but not limited to, a history of data breaches, unencrypted data exposure, questionable transparency, third-party audit concerns, and reports of crypto currency theft due to cracked user vaults exposing users' private keys.

Are there any alternatives?

If you're not using a password manager, passphrases offer better security than passwords and are still easy to remember. While I wouldn't recommend using this one, "My password is P@ssword One Two Three!" is significantly more secure than "P@ssword123" but is just as easy to remember.

2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Add an extra layer of security to your accounts. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA makes it significantly harder for unauthorized access.

3. Be Wary of Phishing Scams

Always double-check the sender of emails and messages. Avoid clicking suspicious links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.

4. Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, web browser, antivirus software, and all applications to patch security vulnerabilities.

5. Use a VPN on Public Wi-Fi

Encrypt your internet connection when using public Wi-Fi networks to protect your data from eavesdropping.

6. Backup Your Data Regularly

Store important files on external drives or cloud services. This protects you against data loss due to cyberattacks, hardware failure, or accidents.

7. Review Privacy Settings

Periodically check and adjust privacy settings on social media, apps, and online services to control who sees your personal information.

8. Secure Your Home Network

Change default router passwords, enable WPA3/WPA2 encryption, and consider creating a guest network.

9. Limit Information Sharing Online

Think twice before sharing sensitive personal information on websites or social media, as it can be used for identity theft or targeted attacks.

10. Monitor Your Accounts

Regularly check bank statements, credit reports, and online account activity for any unusual or unauthorized transactions.

Stay Safe, Stay Informed!

Online security is an ongoing process. Continuously educate yourself on new threats and best practices.
